About

Leadership Rooted in Experience

Fadia Rostom knows what it means to build something from nothing.

She arrived in the United States navigating a new language, a new culture, and a new life — and turned that experience into a career defined by possibility. Today, she is the Founder and Principal Consultant of Vision Scholar Consultation, a K-12 AI education consultancy based in the Berkshires, and one of the region's most sought-after voices on AI, belonging, and leadership.

Before founding Vision Scholar, Fadia served six years as Principal of Saint Agnes Academy in Dalton, MA, where she led the school to the top NEASC accreditation rating among independent schools in Massachusetts. She has taught at Damascus University, and built database systems for international organizations — all before most people were talking about AI in classrooms.

In 2025, she chaired The WE in AI Berkshire Summit, bringing together partners from Harvard, MIT, UMass, and the Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education for the region's first AI education event. Workshops she's facilitated have achieved 85–95% educator AI adoption rates — not because she mandates it, but because she makes it make sense.

Fadia holds a Master of Educational Leadership and Management from Fitchburg State University, a BA from MCLA, and certifications in computer systems engineering. She is a keynote speaker at national conferences including NEASC, DLAC, MATSOL, and FACTS, and a proud advocate for the Hate Has No Home Here movement. She is fluent in Arabic and English, speaks basic Spanish, and is a mother of three daughters.

"At Vision Scholar, the work is personal. It always has been."

FAQ

Common Questions

Do we need existing AI expertise to benefit from your programs?
Not at all. We meet you where you are. Our programs are designed for teams at every level — from AI-curious to AI-confident. Whether you're just starting to explore or looking to deepen existing capabilities, we tailor our approach to your current knowledge and goals.
How long does transformation typically take?
Most clients see immediate results from workshops, with sustained change developing over our 90-day implementation support period. AI integration is a journey, not a destination — we focus on building internal capabilities so your team continues to grow long after our formal engagement ends.
What if our school community is hesitant about AI?
That's exactly why our approach works. We address concerns head-on, demonstrate practical value through hands-on experience, and build confidence gradually. Many of our most successful partnerships started with skeptical teams who became AI champions once they experienced how it enhances — rather than replaces — their work.
What happens after our free consultation?
We'll send you a custom proposal within 48 hours outlining recommended programs, timeline, and investment. There's no pressure — take time to review with your team. When you're ready, we'll schedule your Discovery & Alignment session and transformation begins.
How do you measure success?
Success metrics are defined collaboratively during our Discovery & Alignment session based on your unique goals. Common measures include tool adoption rates, time savings, educator confidence scores, and qualitative feedback. We believe in results that matter to your school — not vanity metrics.
